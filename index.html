"Stay in Salem, Where Every Corner Holds a Story."

  Salem, Massachusetts, is a place steeped in history, famous for the Salem Witch Trials of 1692. The city's rich heritage is not limited to this dark chapter, though; it is also renowned for its maritime history, which can be explored at the Salem Maritime National Historic Site. Visitors can step aboard the Friendship of Salem, a fully operational 1797 merchant vessel, or explore the historic streets with their well-preserved 17th and 18th-century architecture. Beyond history, Salem offers a vibrant cultural scene, with art galleries, museums, and the enchanting Peabody Essex Museum. The city is equally known for its love of all things Halloween, hosting the Salem Haunted Happenings each October, drawing thousands of visitors with its spooky attractions. Salem is a captivating blend of the past and the present, where history, culture, and a touch of mystique come together to create a unique and captivating destination.
